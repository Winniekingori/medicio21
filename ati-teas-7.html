What is the ATI TEAS 7 Exam?

The ATI TEAS 7 (Test of Essential Academic Skills) is a standardized test designed to assess your readiness for nursing school. It evaluates your abilities in four core areas:

Reading

Measures comprehension, passage analysis, and the ability to synthesize information.

Mathematics

Tests arithmetic, algebra, data interpretation, and critical problem-solving skills.

Science

Focuses on anatomy, physiology, biology, chemistry, and scientific reasoning.

English and Language Usage

Assesses grammar, sentence structure, and language conventions.

ATI TEAS 7 Exam Format and Structure

Understanding the test format is vital for effective preparation. Here’s a breakdown of the ATI TEAS 7 exam structure:

Section Number of Questions Time Limit What It Covers
Reading 45 55 minutes Comprehension, analysis of texts, and synthesizing key ideas
Mathematics 38 57 minutes Numbers, algebra, measurement, data representation
Science 50 60 minutes Anatomy, physiology, biology, chemistry, and scientific reasoning
English & Language Usage 37 37 minutes Grammar, punctuation, sentence structure, and vocabulary

The total test duration is 209 minutes, with 170 questions (including 20 unscored pretest items).

Content Breakdown for Each Section

  • Reading
  • Mathematics
  • Science
  • English and Language Usage

Reading

The Reading section requires strong comprehension and critical thinking skills. You’ll encounter questions about:

  • Summarizing key ideas in passages.
  • Analyzing text structures and relationships between ideas.
  • Synthesizing information from multiple sources.

Pro Tip: Focus on identifying the author’s purpose and main idea quickly. Practice skimming and scanning techniques for longer passages.

Mathematics

The Math section tests fundamental arithmetic, algebra, and data analysis. Topics include:

  • Fractions, percentages, and decimals.
  • Solving equations and inequalities.
  • Interpreting graphs and tables.

Pro Tip:Memorize basic formulas and practice solving problems without a calculator for added confidence.

Science

This section delves into your knowledge of anatomy, physiology, biology, and chemistry. Key areas include:

  • Understanding body systems and their functions.
  • Interpreting scientific data and experiments.
  • Applying principles of biology and chemistry.

Pro Tip: Create flashcards for complex terms and processes to reinforce your understanding.

English and Language Usage

In this section, your grammar and sentence structure skills are tested. Topics include:

  • Punctuation, spelling, and capitalization rules.
  • Sentence structure and syntax.
  • Vocabulary and word usage.

Pro Tip: Read widely to familiarize yourself with different writing styles and improve your language skills naturally.
